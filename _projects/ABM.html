Overview

Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) is a powerful computational approach where individual entities (agents) interact inside a dynamic environment. These interactions shape the emergent behaviour of the entire system, enabling simulation of complex social, biological, and behavioural processes that cannot be captured with traditional equation-based models.

ðŸ§  Technical Highlights

The ABM framework utilizes:

  • State machines to model human daily activity patterns (home, work, transit).
  • Graph-theoretic interactions to simulate contact networks and exposure risk.
  • Patch-based vector population models for mosquito dynamics (temperature, humidity, rainfall).
  • Probabilistic transmission models for airborne & vector-borne diseases.

This allows simulation of interventions such as:

  • School/workplace closures
  • Targeted quarantine
  • Vaccination rollouts
  • Fogging / hotspot treatment for vector control
