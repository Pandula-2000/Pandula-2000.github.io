1. Overview

Agent-Based Modeling (ABM) is a powerful computational approach where individual entities (agents) interact within a dynamic environment. These interactions shape the emergent behaviour of the whole system, enabling simulation of complex social, biological, and behavioural processes that cannot be captured with traditional equation-based models.

My work in ABM focuses on integrating real-world human mobility data, AI-driven behavioural clustering, and stochastic simulation to model public health systems—particularly airborne diseases (COVID-19) and vector-borne diseases (dengue).

3. Key Features of the Project

  • Real-world GPS mobility modeling from 100+ voluntary participants.
  • Use of Spectral Clustering and DBSCAN to identify behavioural patterns.
  • Simulation of COVID-19 and dengue at high spatial and behavioural fidelity.
  • Transportation-aware disease spread across buses, public transit, and crowded hubs.
  • Evaluation of quarantine, vaccination, and vector-control policies.
  • Hybrid integration with SEIR compartment models for robust epidemiological accuracy.

4. Technical Highlights

The AVSim framework incorporates:

  • State-machine models for daily human activity patterns.
  • Graph-theoretic contact networks for exposure modeling.
  • Patch-based vector ecology for mosquito population dynamics (temperature, rainfall, humidity).
  • Probabilistic transmission models for airborne and vector-borne infections.
  • AI-driven behavioural clustering to infer mobility and contact subtypes.
